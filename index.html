About MOA

Not a
vendor.
A system.

MOA is a brand experience and UX/UI design practice built around one conviction: design is infrastructure. Not decoration. Not aesthetics for their own sake. Structure that scales.

I work with startups building for scale, product-led companies, and teams experiencing visual inconsistency — founders who understand the difference between a vendor and a strategic partner.

Every engagement starts with a question: does this require a system? If the answer is no, MOA is not the right fit. If the answer is yes, we build something that lasts.

MOA does not sell visuals. MOA sells clarity, structure, and long-term usability.

How MOA Works

No steps
are
skipped.

01
Inquiry & Qualification
GO / NO-GO Filter
Every project is filtered through a qualification checklist. It must require a system, have a clear decision-maker, a realistic timeline, and a budget aligned with structural work. If more than two criteria fail — MOA declines.
02
Proposal & Contract
Scope, Timeline, Investment
A clear proposal with defined scope. 50% upfront, 50% on final delivery. Work pauses if payments pause. Files are released after final payment. Professional boundaries protect quality — on both sides.
03
Discovery & Audit
Understanding Before Building
Before a single component is designed, we audit what exists. What's broken. What's missing. What the team has tried to patch. Structure and strategy come from this — not assumptions.
04
System Build
Structured. Documented. Explained.
The build phase follows the strategy. Everything is documented. If it cannot be explained clearly, it is not ready. Feedback is consolidated — no design-by-committee, no real-time debates.
05
Handoff & Follow-up
Delivered. Transferred. Closed.
Guidelines, documentation, and a structured handoff. No undocumented systems leave MOA. A post-project follow-up ensures the system is being used as intended. Consistency compounds authority.
